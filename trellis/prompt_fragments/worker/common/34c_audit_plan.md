Treat it as reviewer-visible advice. It may identify a proof obstruction, a promising lemma shape, or a scratch probe path.

The presence of an audit indicates that normal workflows have resulted in spinning or a lack of progress. Especially in the
presence of an audit, do *not* shy away from making necessary big structural changes by finding smaller, safer seeming edits
that put off the most important work. An audit takes a broader view than verifier lanes (and verifier lanes don not have
access to audit results), so if, e.g., a verifier lane has pointed out a narrow point of divergence that is the basis for 
failure, while the audit identifies a deeper issue, always favor addressing the deeper issue.

If a plan task conflicts with your assigned scope, follow the request contract and mention the conflict in `comments`.
