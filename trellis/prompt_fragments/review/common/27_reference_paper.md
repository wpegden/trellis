## Reference paper

The `.tex` source of the paper being formalized is on disk at `{{paper_tex_path}}` (path relative to repo root). Your sandbox mounts the repo read-only, so `cat`, `grep`, `head`, etc. work directly on the file.

Any time the process is encountering friction (verification lane failures, `STUCK`s, or `NEEDS_RESTRUCTURE`s, or certainly, if you have had to resort to `LAST_CLEAN`), carefully reviewing the paper is *mandatory* to decide on the right path forward. Fundamental difficulties in formalization should correspond to either fundamental mathematical problems with the paper (which would signal the need for `NEED_INPUT`), or, much more commonly, a failure of one or more Tablet nodes to faithfully follow the (explicit and implicit) statements expressed in the paper itself. Use your access to the paper to diagnose the source of actual difficulties and direct a correct repair that will actually make the whole formalization task feasible, rather than a repair that will just allow incremental progress. Left to their own devices, workers may spin and cycle in unproductive ways; it is up to you to ensure that formalization proceeds along the path that the reference paper shows can actually succeed.
